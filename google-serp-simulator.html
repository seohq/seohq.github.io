Common Questions

Frequently Asked
Questions

How long should a Google title tag be? +
Google typically displays 50–60 characters before truncating. The real limit is pixel-based (~600px). Keep titles under 60 characters, include your primary keyword early, and add your brand at the end separated by | or –.
How long should a meta description be? +
Target 120–158 characters for desktop. On mobile Google sometimes trims to ~120 chars. Aim for the 140–155 sweet spot. Note: Google frequently rewrites descriptions if it judges yours doesn't match the page content well enough.
Does the meta description affect rankings? +
Meta descriptions are not a direct ranking factor — Google has confirmed this. However, a compelling description significantly impacts your CTR. Higher CTR sends positive engagement signals that can indirectly influence rankings over time.
What are rich snippets and how do I get them? +
Rich snippets are enhanced results with extra info — star ratings, FAQ dropdowns, video thumbnails, prices. Implement Schema.org structured data (JSON-LD) on your page. Google then decides whether to show it. Use Google's Rich Results Test to validate your markup.
Is this simulator 100% accurate to Google? +
This provides a close approximation, but Google's actual display depends on device, screen resolution, the search query, and whether Google rewrites your title or description dynamically. Use it for planning, then verify with Google Search Console after publishing.
What is a good CTR for Google search results? +
Position 1 averages ~28–32% CTR, position 2 drops to ~15–17%, position 3 ~10–11%, and position 10 falls below 3%. Rich snippets and strong meta descriptions can significantly boost CTR above average for your position.

About This Tool

What is a
SERP Simulator?

The Tool

The SEO HQ Google SERP Simulator lets you preview exactly how your page appears in Google's search results before publishing. It simulates the title tag, meta description, URL breadcrumb, rich snippets, and sitelinks in a pixel-accurate Google-style interface — in both desktop and mobile views.

Why It Matters

Your Google search snippet is your organic ad. It determines whether someone clicks your result or your competitor's. A well-crafted title and description can double your CTR without changing your ranking. Iterate quickly and confidently with live feedback.

Key Features

  • Real-time live preview as you type
  • Colour-coded character counter feedback
  • Snippet quality scoring and breakdown

  • One-click copy of ready-to-use meta HTML

Best Practices

  • Write descriptions for humans, not bots
