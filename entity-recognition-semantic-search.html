Entity Recognition and Semantic Search

Published: Jan 16, 2026 • 9 min read • By SEO HQ

Modern search engines have evolved from matching keywords (strings) to understanding concepts (things). This transition is powered by Named Entity Recognition (NER) and Semantic Search, forming the backbone of the modern Knowledge Graph.

1. What is Named Entity Recognition (NER)?

NER is a sub-task of information extraction that seeks to locate and classify atomic elements in text into predefined categories such as names of persons, organizations, locations, and even abstract concepts. By identifying these entities, search engines can understand the subject matter of a page with high confidence.

2. The Role of Semantic Search

Semantic search seeks to improve search accuracy by understanding the searcher's intent and the contextual meaning of terms. Instead of looking for exact word matches, it looks for the relationship between entities. For example, a search for "the director of Inception" identifies the entity "Inception" (movie) and the relationship "Director" to return "Christopher Nolan."

3. From Strings to Things

When Google processes a query, it uses semantic triples (Subject-Predicate-Object) to build a web of knowledge. This allows the engine to provide direct answers in SERP features, like Knowledge Panels and Featured Snippets, because it "knows" the facts rather than just finding pages that mention them.

"Entities are the new keywords. If search engines can't identify the unique subjects of your content, they can't accurately categorize your authority."

Optimizing for Entity-Based Search

  • Structured Data: Use Schema.org markup to explicitly define entities (Person, Organization, Event) to help search engines parse your data.
  • Topical Relevancy: Surround your main entity with related attributes and secondary entities to provide clear context.
  • Internal Linking: Use descriptive anchor text that reinforces the relationship between different entity-focused pages on your site.
