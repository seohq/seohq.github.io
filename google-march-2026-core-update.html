Update Overview & Rollout Timeline

Google officially confirmed the March 2026 Core Update on March 27, 2026. This is the first broad core update of the year and is expected to take up to two weeks to fully roll out.

  • Start date: March 27, 2026 (2:00 AM PT)
  • Expected completion: Early April 2026
  • Scope: Global, all languages, all industries, all site types
  • Type: Broad core update (not spam-focused like the February update)

Early data from tracking tools shows unusually high SERP volatility — one of the strongest movements seen so far in 2026.

What Actually Changed in This Update

Google has not published an official blog post detailing the goals, but real-time signals and industry analysis point to stronger emphasis on:

  • Deeper E-E-A-T evaluation (especially first-hand experience and authoritativeness)
  • Helpful content signals and user engagement metrics
  • Reduced reliance on exact keyword matching in favor of topical depth
  • Stronger demotion of thin, AI-generated, or low-value content
  • Better alignment with AI Overviews and generative search results
