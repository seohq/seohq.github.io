Common Questions

Frequently Asked
Questions

What meta tags are most important for SEO? +
The most impactful meta tags for SEO are: the title tag (strong ranking signal), meta description (affects CTR), canonical tag (prevents duplicate content), and robots meta (controls indexation). Open Graph and Twitter Card tags don't directly affect rankings but influence click-through from social media.
Does the meta keywords tag still work? +
No. Google officially stopped using the meta keywords tag in 2009 and Bing followed. Including it won't hurt you, but it provides zero SEO benefit. Your time is better spent on title, description, and structured data. Some minor or regional search engines may still read it.
What is the difference between og:title and the title tag? +
The HTML title tag controls the text shown in search results and browser tabs. og:title controls the text shown when your page is shared on social platforms like Facebook, LinkedIn, and messaging apps. Best practice is to keep them the same or very similar — diverge only if the social context benefits from a different framing.
What size should the Open Graph image be? +
The recommended OG image size is 1200×630 pixels at a minimum. Facebook recommends at least 600×315px, but 1200×630px ensures sharp display on high-density screens. Keep your file under 8MB. PNG or JPEG both work. Avoid embedding important text near the edges as platforms may crop.
When should I use noindex? +
Use noindex on: thank-you / confirmation pages, admin or login pages, duplicate content (prefer canonical instead), thin or low-value pages, internal search result pages, and staging or test environments. Never noindex your homepage, key landing pages, or blog posts you want ranked.
Do Twitter Card tags work on LinkedIn too? +
LinkedIn primarily reads Open Graph tags (og:), not Twitter Card tags. Twitter/X reads both but prioritises its own twitter: tags. The best approach is to always set complete OG tags as your base, and add Twitter Card tags on top. This ensures full coverage across all major platforms.
